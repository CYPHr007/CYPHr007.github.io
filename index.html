NOUS

From predictive maintenance to maintenance intelligence.

My first business started as PILAR and became NOUS. The question that started it stayed the same: if machines show signs before failure, why are those signs still missed? The answer has grown. NOUS is no longer just a model that predicts. It is becoming the memory layer for a plant, every recommendation, every decision, and every outcome, recorded, and built for any industrial machine.

NOUS started from what I saw on the industrial side: signal before failure, missed every time. That part still holds. What changed is the scope. NOUS is machine-agnostic, it retrains on any equipment's real data, and it now carries the decisions a team makes, not just the warnings it gives. From reactive to structured is the short version of where this is going.

The system matters to me because it is not abstract. It connects software, machine signals, failure patterns, and the people who actually need to decide whether something is becoming dangerous. Confidence that is earned, not assumed, even when that means the system saying it does not know yet.

Human Archive

A short node, still on the map.

Human Archive gets its own section even though I was only there about a month. In that time it pushed me further into hardware and motion capture, and into the idea that model quality starts much earlier, at the level of how data is physically collected.

Human Archive builds hardware rigs for full-body motion capture, recording how people actually move so that data can be used to train robots. Around it, the work became more physical: rigs, motion, synchronization, and what it takes to collect data that is actually good enough to matter downstream.

I moved on after about a month, back to NOUS, but the shift in how I think about data collection, that quality starts at the rig and the sensor, not in the model, stayed with me.
