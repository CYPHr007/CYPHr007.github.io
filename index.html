// my journey

From curiosity to creation

From gaming all day in Morocco to founding a startup in Shenzhen. Here's how it happened.

Morocco

The gamer years

Growing up in El Jadida, Morocco, I had zero connection to the tech world. I was a huge video games player; that was my entire universe. I graduated from a French high school not knowing what a line of code looked like. Tech wasn't even on my radar. But looking back, gaming taught me something: the patience to grind through hard problems and the obsession to get better.

Summit of Mount Toubkal, 4,167m. I was 16.
The Seed

Internship at Kapachim Maroc

Before I knew anything about code, I did an internship at Kapachim Maroc, a chemical manufacturing plant. I saw the factory floor up close: the machines, the breakdowns, the cost of unplanned downtime. I didn't know it yet, but this experience planted the seed that would later become PILAR. The problem was real, I just didn't have the tools to solve it. Not yet.

Internship at Kapachim Maroc. Where the idea for PILAR was born.
The Leap

Leaving home at 18

At 18, I left my parents and everything I knew in Morocco to move to Shenzhen, China, alone, on the other side of the world. No safety net, no connections in tech, barely any Mandarin. I enrolled in Computer Science and Engineering at CUHK Shenzhen. The distance from family was the hardest part, but I knew that if I wanted to build something real, I had to put myself where things are built.

First day in Shenzhen. A new chapter.
The Brotherhood

Dorm life and the people who changed everything

I moved into a small dorm room with 3 other students. They became my brothers. When you're far from home, the people around you become your family. We studied together, struggled together, and pushed each other forward. And then there's Rayan, the friend who told me to apply to CUHK Shenzhen in the first place. He introduced me to the tech world, connected me with people in the field, and opened my eyes to the founding and startup ecosystem. I wouldn't be here without him.

With Rayan. The one who started it all.
The Transformation

Zero to everything in under a year

Shenzhen changed everything. In less than a year, I went from someone who had never coded to someone building full-stack apps, training ML models, and writing AI agents. I absorbed everything: Python, web development, machine learning, desktop app packaging, system design. The speed surprised even me. When you're surrounded by innovation and have nothing to fall back on, you learn fast.

The Build

Creating PILAR

All of it clicked into one idea: what if machines could tell you they were about to break, before they did? That question became PILAR. I mastered generative AI and used it as my superpower: leveraging LLMs to architect the system, build the models, design the interface, and create the AI agents. One person, powered by the right tools, building an industrial-grade product. No funding, no team, just relentless iteration fueled by the same obsessive energy that once went into gaming.

Now

Shipping, studying, and scaling

PILAR is live: a desktop app with local AI agents, offline-first, zero cloud dependency. Built by one person who learned to wield generative AI as a force multiplier. I'm still a CSE student at CUHK Shenzhen, still learning Chinese (the most spoken language in the world), and still building. I speak French, Arabic, and English fluently. The journey from a gamer in Morocco to a startup founder in China happened in under a year, but it's only the beginning.

// the product

PILAR

Predictive Industrial Maintenance. AI that predicts machine failures before they happen. Fully offline, fully local, no cloud required.

ML-Powered Predictions

XGBoost model trained on 236k+ data points across 23 machine profiles. Predicts failures with calibrated probability scores.

Local AI Agents

Three specialized agents (diagnostic, maintenance, and alert) powered by Ollama running LLMs entirely on your machine. No internet needed.

Desktop-First

Ships as a native Windows installer. Built with PyInstaller and Inno Setup. Runs in your system tray, works without a browser.

Privacy by Design

Zero cloud dependency. All data stays on-premise. No telemetry, no external calls. Your factory data never leaves your network.
